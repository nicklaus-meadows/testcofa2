Understanding the Certificate of Analysis (CoA)

What Does a CoA Include?

  • Product Information: Name, GFS Chemicals' batch number, and manufacturer details.
  • Testing Parameters: Lists the tests conducted, such as potency, purity, and contaminants.
  • Results: Quantitative results for each test, often with reference to acceptable limits.
  • Compliance Statement: Confirmation that the product meets regulatory requirements.
  • Signature: An authorized representativeâ€™s signature, affirming the accuracy of the information.

Importance of CoA

  • Quality Assurance: Ensures products meet safety and efficacy standards.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Essential for adhering to industry regulations and legal requirements.
  • Consumer Trust: Builds confidence among consumers regarding product safety and quality.
